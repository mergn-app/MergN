## What is allowed

- Run MergN on your own infrastructure (self-host).
- Use MergN in production for your own company operations.
- Offer services built with MergN for your clients, as long as you are not
  providing MergN itself (or a substantially similar platform) as a hosted
  product.
- Modify the code for your own use.
- Fork and publish your modifications under the same license terms.

## What is not allowed without a commercial license

- Offering MergN (or a derivative) as a hosted/managed automation platform.
- White-labeling MergN (or a derivative) as your own automation product.
- Embedding MergN as the core automation engine of a commercial product where
  end users consume MergN-like platform capabilities.
- Operating a competing workflow automation SaaS built from this codebase.

## Does this remain open source?

`BUSL-1.1` is source-available, not OSI open source.
